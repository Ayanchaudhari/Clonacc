History

Biometric technology has been evolving for decades, starting with fingerprint recognition in the late 19th century. Over time, advancements in computer technology made it possible to store and analyze biometric data more effectively. The use of biometrics for attendance systems gained popularity in the early 21st century as organizations sought secure and accurate ways to track employee attendance.

About

The Biometric Attendance System is a modern solution designed to automate the process of recording attendance using unique physical or behavioral characteristics of individuals, such as fingerprints, facial recognition, or iris scans. This system eliminates the possibility of proxy attendance and enhances workplace security and efficiency.

Benefits

  • Accurate and reliable attendance tracking.
  • Improved security by ensuring only authorized individuals gain access.
  • Eliminates the need for manual record-keeping, reducing errors.
  • Saves time and enhances organizational efficiency.
  • Real-time attendance monitoring and reporting.
